About

I'm a data scientist and programmer with a PhD in Political Science (Quantitative Methods) from Emory University. With over 13 years of experience in analytics and programming, I work at the intersection of statistics, software engineering, and research communication.

Currently at Atorus Research, I build open-source R packages and interactive tools for clinical trial data monitoring — translating complex statistical frameworks into software that's actually useful. Before that, I led a 9-person data science team at Morning Consult and conducted academic research at the Varieties of Democracy Institute in Gothenburg, Sweden.

I care about making data genuinely understandable — whether through clean code, well-designed charts, or research that reaches real audiences.
